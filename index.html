About me

Hi there. I’m Katherine.

I thrive at the crossroads of curiosity and analysis, seeking to see how far I can push the envelope to make fresh and intriguing products. I’m particularly interested in tech softwares and hardwares, but also love to tinker in other fields. I'm currently a senior at Northwestern University bridging studies in business, psychology, and product design.

One thing you can count on me to talk about for hours is: GPUs - what's with ray-tracing and AI, anyway? But, beyond that, I'm a cartography lover who draws maps in my free time. What's more? I'm currently working with a team to build a historically accurate immersive virtual map of Manhattan throughout history.

TimeWalk: Ships Through History

This project explores the evolution of maritime vessels from ancient galleys to modern container ships.

The Age of Sail

From the 16th to the 19th century, sailing ships dominated maritime trade and naval warfare. This visualization recreates historically accurate models of ships from this era.

Based on naval architectural plans from maritime museums and historical records, these models showcase the evolution of ship design and seafaring technology.

Port of Charleston: 1783

This detailed recreation of Charleston Harbor in 1783 shows the bustling maritime activity during the early years of American independence, with accurate ship models based on historical records.

Industrial Era Vessels

The transition from sail to steam power revolutionized maritime transportation. This series explores the technological advancements of 19th and early 20th century ships.

Using engineering documents and photographs from maritime archives, these models demonstrate the industrial evolution of seafaring vessels.
